# Zensical ways of working

Use this file when the document should reflect how Zensical approaches
collaboration, design, and decision-making.

## Default stance

- Build with users, not just for users.
- Make priorities and movement legible through navigable source-of-truth
  documents.
- Frame discussion around opportunities, trade-offs, and design choices instead
  of only feature requests or implementation details.
- Keep the path from request to proposal to design to implementation visible.

## What this means for documentation

- Make the reader's decision space explicit. Say what problem exists, what is
  in scope, and what is out of scope.
- Preserve rationale. If a choice was made, record why and what alternatives
  were considered.
- Keep references connected. Link change requests, backlog items, roadmap
  entries, proposals, design docs, and implementation docs when they exist.
- Optimize for low-noise discoverability. Readers should know where the current
  truth lives after reading the page.
- Invite feedback at intentional points instead of scattering discussion
  surfaces everywhere.

## Tone and structure implications

- Write with confidence, but show the thinking.
- Prefer high-signal summaries followed by evidence or detail.
- Use diagrams and tables to compress complexity without hiding trade-offs.
- Keep process pages concrete: who acts, what changes state, and where the next
  step happens.
- Keep issue triage and backlog references tidy. Do not create multiple
  competing "current" documents.

## Questions to answer in process or design docs

1. What opportunity or problem triggered this work?
2. Who is affected and how?
3. What alternatives were explored?
4. Why is the chosen direction better now?
5. What risks, constraints, or open questions remain?
6. Where should readers go next to track progress or contribute feedback?
